\caption{\textbf{``Fully good'' review items: descriptive statistics and paired comparisons.}
A review item is \emph{fully good} if it is rated Correct, Significant (encoded value 2, including the original 4-option ``Very Significant'' choice), \emph{and} Sufficient on the evidence axis.
\textbf{Top:} per-group descriptive rates with two complementary aggregations.
The \emph{paper-level mean} is the average across papers of the per-paper fully-good fraction (each paper weighted equally), with a $t$-interval 95\% CI.
The \emph{item-level rate} pools all items together (overall fully-good count divided by total items) and uses the Wilson score 95\% CI.
The two summaries are close but not identical: the paper-level mean weights each paper equally regardless of how many items it contributes, while the item-level rate weights papers in proportion to their item count.
The ``Fully Good'' and ``Total Items'' columns show the underlying counts.
\textbf{Bottom:} all 10 pairwise paired comparisons on the per-paper fully-good rate.
Each row reports the paired difference (row $-$ column), its 95\% CI, Cohen's $d$, and the raw $p$-value from a paired $t$-test on per-paper rate differences.
A positive entry indicates the first-named reviewer has a higher fully-good rate than the second-named reviewer.
$^{*}p < 0.05$, $^{**}p < 0.01$, $^{***}p < 0.001$; unadjusted for multiple comparisons.
