\caption{\textbf{Mixed-effects logistic regression confirms that AI reviewers raise more significant issues but are less factually correct than the Top-Rated Human reviewer.}
Coefficients are on the \emph{log-odds} (logit) scale from a Bayesian binomial generalized linear mixed model (\texttt{BinomialBayesMixedGLM}, variational Bayes, paper random intercept) for correctness and evidence, and from a binomial GLMM at the ``Significant'' cut point ($P(Y = 2)$) for the ordinal significance score.
The two halves of the table refit the same model with a different reference category so that all pairwise contrasts to either the Top-Rated or Lowest-Rated Human reviewer can be read directly off a single row.
A positive coefficient means the row reviewer has higher log-odds of the outcome than the reference; convert to a probability change at the reference via $\sigma(\text{intercept}+\text{coef})-\sigma(\text{intercept})$ where $\sigma$ is the logistic function.
Standard errors in parentheses.
$^{*}p < 0.05$, $^{**}p < 0.01$, $^{***}p < 0.001$ (raw $p$-values from the posterior, unadjusted for multiple comparisons).
ICC is computed on the latent log-odds scale via $\sigma_u^2/(\sigma_u^2 + \pi^2/3)$ and represents the share of variance attributable to between-paper differences.
