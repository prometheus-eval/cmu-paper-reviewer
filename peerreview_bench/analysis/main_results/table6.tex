\caption{\textbf{Annotator-judged win rates: fraction of papers on which each AI reviewer ``matches or exceeds'' the top-rated and lowest-rated human reviewer.}
After reading all three human and all three AI reviews for a paper, each expert annotator was asked two checkbox questions in the per-paper survey: ``Compared to the best human review, which AI-generated reviews match or exceed that level of quality?'' and the parallel question against the worst human review.
Each survey response contributes one Bernoulli observation per AI model per baseline.
The 85 primary and 27 secondary (overlap-paper) annotators give $n = 112$ observations per cell.
Rates are the pooled proportion of wins across all 112 observations; 95\% confidence intervals are a non-parametric cluster bootstrap with 10{,}000 resamples, clustering on HF \texttt{paper\_id} to account for within-paper correlation on the overlap papers.
Under this convention, GPT-5.2 matches or exceeds the top-rated human on $\sim$49\% of papers and the lowest-rated human on $\sim$73\%; Claude Opus 4.5 and Gemini 3.0 Pro sit noticeably lower on the top-rated comparison ($\sim$30--32\%) but recover on the lowest-rated comparison ($\sim$59--68\%), consistent with the correctness-vs-significance trade-off highlighted in Tables~\ref{tab:main-results} and~\ref{tab:glmm}.
